Know What You're Looking At
🧪 pH Level
pH measures how acidic or basic the water is. Most fish thrive between 6.5 and 8.5. Extreme values — too low (acid rain, pollution) or too high (algae blooms) — stress fish and kill spawning beds.
✓ What to look for: Healthy water is clear with no unusual smell. Green or brown scum on the surface can signal a pH spike from algae.
Test with strips
⚠️ Dissolved Oxygen
Fish breathe oxygen dissolved in water. Below 5 mg/L fish become stressed. Below 3 mg/L it is a fish kill event. Hot summer days, heavy algae, and runoff from fields all drop oxygen fast.
⚠️ Warning sign: Fish gasping at the surface early morning. Dead fish near shore. Report it immediately.
Critical indicator
👁️ Water Clarity
Clarity tells you how far light penetrates. Murky water from runoff, boat traffic, or algae blocks sunlight from aquatic plants fish depend on. A Secchi disk reading below 12 inches indicates poor conditions.
✓ DIY test: Lower a white object on a string. Note the depth where it disappears. Under 12 inches = report it.
Easy to test
🚨 Fishing Line & Trash
Monofilament line takes 600 years to decompose. It entangles birds, turtles, and fish. Plastic bags mimic jellyfish and are eaten by catfish and bass. Trash left at Carlyle Lake is one of the biggest threats to the fishery right now.
🚨 Action: Carry a small bag. Pick up 3 pieces of trash every trip. Use monofilament recycling tubes at the marina.
Report violations
🌿 Invasive Species
Asian carp, zebra mussels, and hydrilla weed are active threats in Southern Illinois waters. They outcompete native species, destroy spawning habitat, and cost millions to manage once established.
⚠️ What to do: Clean your boat, drain your livewell, dry your gear before moving between waters. Report unusual fish or plants immediately.
IDNR reportable
🌡️ Water Temperature
Temperature drives everything — fish metabolism, spawn timing, oxygen levels, and bait activity. Knowing your water temp tells you where fish are holding and what they will hit. It also signals environmental stress early.
✓ Use BaitLogic USGS data above for live readings. Best fishing: bass at 60–75°F, crappie at 55–68°F, catfish at 70–85°F.

Clean Up Crew

Carlyle Lake is one of the best fisheries in Illinois — and right now it is drowning in fishing line, plastic bags, and trash left by people who should know better. BaitLogic is organizing the community to take it back.

The Problem at Carlyle Lake Right Now
🪢 Abandoned Fishing Line
Monofilament and braided line left on banks, in trees, and in the water. It kills birds, turtles, and fish every single season at Carlyle. Takes 600 years to break down.
→ Always cut line into small pieces before disposal. Never leave it on the bank or in the water.
🗑️ Trash on the Banks
Plastic bags, bait containers, food wrappers, and bottles left at access points and along the shoreline. It enters the water and is consumed by the fish we are trying to catch and protect.
→ Pack it in, pack it out. Carry a bag. Pick up 3 pieces every trip — takes 30 seconds.
⚠️ Illegal Dumping Near Access Points
Old tires, appliances, and bulk waste dumped near boat ramps and parking areas. It leaches chemicals directly into the lake and discourages responsible anglers from using the area.
→ Report illegal dumping to Madison/Clinton County at (618) 825-2010 or IL EPA.

Founder · Builder · Angler

About BaitLogic

🎣
Amber
Founder · BaitLogic™
📍 Highland / Collinsville, IL · Southern Illinois

I am a self-taught angler who got tired of showing up to the water without the right information. Tired of apps that were built for everyone — which means they were really built for no one. Tired of fishing communities where the same questions got asked over and over because the right tools never existed for everyday people on the water.

I built BaitLogic from my phone — no computer, no development team, no outside funding. Just a vision, a Galaxy S26, and a deep belief that anglers in Southern Illinois and the St. Louis Metro deserve a platform as serious as the fishing culture we already have here.

I am also a rockhound, photographer, and conservationist who finds specimens along the Mississippi River. The water is not just where I fish — it is where I think, explore, and find peace. Watching it get trashed, degraded, and ignored is what made the Guardian and Clean Up Crew programs personal to me. BaitLogic is not just an app. It is a movement to protect the waters we love.

The Mission — Three Pillars
🎣 Community First
BaitLogic exists for the bank fisher who cannot afford a boat. For the kid picking up a rod for the first time. For the underserved communities that have always fished but never had a voice in the technology built around this sport. Free forever. No gatekeeping. No paywalls on essential fishing information.
📡 Real Intelligence
Live water temperature from USGS government sensors. Real-time NOAA weather tied to your exact water. Community bite reports cross-referenced with conditions data. Depth charts and structure maps from public IDNR data. The information was always out there — BaitLogic puts it in one place, in plain language, before you leave the house.
🌊 Conservation Always
The Waterway Health Index and Clean Up Crew are not features — they are the reason this platform exists. Carlyle Lake is one of the finest fisheries in Illinois and it is being slowly destroyed by trash, abandoned fishing line, and indifference. BaitLogic organizes the people who care enough to do something about it.
